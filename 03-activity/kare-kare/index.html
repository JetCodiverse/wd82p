Kare-kare

Kare-Kare, a traditional Filipino stew made with oxtail, vegetables, and a peanut-based sauce.
Kare-Kare is usually served with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) on the side. Enjoy!

Ingredients:

For the stew:

  • 2 lbs (about 1 kg) oxtail, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 lb (about 500g) tripe (optional), cleaned and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon annatto powder or 2 tablespoons annatto seeds (dissolved in 3 tablespoons water) - for color (optional)
  • 4 cups beef or bone broth
  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced diagonally
  • 1 bunch bok choy or pechay (Chinese cabbage)
  • 1/2 pound string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the shrimp paste (bagoong):

  • 1/2 cup bagoong alamang (fermented shrimp paste)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional, for a touch of sweetness)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the shrimp paste (bagoong): In a small pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Saute the chopped onions and garlic until the onions are translucent. Add the bagoong alamang and cook for a few minutes until the mixture is heated through. If desired, add a touch of brown sugar for a slight sweetness. Remove from heat and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Sear the oxtail and tripe (if using) until browned on all sides. Remove the meat from the pot and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, saut√© the chopped onions and minced garlic until fragrant and translucent. If using annatto seeds, add them with the water and let them simmer to extract the color. If using annatto powder, skip this step.
  4. Return the oxtail and tripe (if using) to the pot. Pour in the beef or bone broth, and bring it to a boil. Lower the heat to a simmer, cover the pot, and let it cook for about 2 to 3 hours or until the oxtail and tripe are tender. You can also use a pressure cooker to reduce the cooking time.
  5. Once the meat is tender, add the peanut butter to the pot, stirring it into the broth until well combined. Let it simmer for an additional 10 minutes or until the sauce thickens. If the sauce is too thick, you can add more broth to reach your desired consistency.
  6. Add the sliced eggplant, string beans, and bok choy (or pechay) to the pot. Continue to simmer until the vegetables are cooked but still slightly crisp. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the Kare-Kare hot with steamed rice and the prepared shrimp paste on the side. Enjoy this flavorful Filipino stew with family and friends!

Note: If you're short on time, you can also use pre-made Kare-Kare mix available in some specialty stores. However, preparing the stew from scratch with fresh ingredients will give you the best flavor.
